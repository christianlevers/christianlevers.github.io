Bachelor and Master theses

Here are some potential thesis topics. Also own suggestions are welcome. Please regard the attributes Master and Bachelor thesis as a suggestion - topics, scope and tasks are negotiable and up to your creativity. I also indicate the skills you will acquire during the thesis - previous knowledge is welcome but not mandatory.

Bachelor/Master thesis: Ecosystem functions provided by animals and potential disruptions under global change

Animals provide important ecosystem functions to each other. For example, ants and birds disperse seeds and nutrients, and (wild)bees and butterflies pollinate plants. Some of these mutualistic interactions may be highly specialised, for example when one plant species is only pollinated by one insect species, other interactions may be more generalistic. Here, we will compile a list of important ecosystem functions by animals, and assess the vulnerability of these under global environmental change.

  • Skills learned: Literature search, meta-analyses, species distribution modelling
Master thesis: Life history strategies in birds: traits as proxy of intra- and interspecific interactions

Life history emerges from natural selection. Individuals have to trade-off costs and benefits of specific actions, how much they invest in reproduction, survival, maintenance and defence. Here, we will analyse correlations between life-history traits in birds (e.g. body size, clutch size, diet) and their competitive behaviours towards conspecifics and heterospecifics. This will help us to better understand how trait syndromes may mediate interspecific interactions. We will analyse Swiss breeding birds for which we already have extensive data on foraging and habitat traits available. These trait data will be extended by information on territorial/competitive behaviours that will be obtained from literature research.

  • Skills learned: R programming, statistical analyses, trait analyses
Master thesis: Improving winter occurrence data for migratory birds

Migratory birds use distant habitats during breeding and overwintering. Existing range maps on wintering habitats are usually less accurate than the range maps of the breeding season. Here, we will use citizen-science based occurrence records from eBird to improve distribution maps of migratory birds, focussing on long-distance migrants breeding in Europe and overwintering in Africa. Species distribution models will be estimated using range maps and using eBird data, and inference will be compared.

  • Skills learned: R programming, GIS analyses, species distribution modelling
Master thesis: Integrating consumer-resource interactions in bird species distribution models

Birds (and other animals) are not only influenced by abiotic conditions such as climate but also by the amount of available resources. Often, resources such as the favourite food plants are included in the models only indirectly through accounting for major habitat associations or correlations with climate. Here, we will build a hierarchical model in which the occurrence of the bird and the occurrence of its food plants is modelled simultaneously. This will allow disentangling the abiotic effects on food plants from the abiotic effects on consumer survival. I would suggest using black grouse as study species, but this is not mandatory.

  • Skills learned: R programming, GIS analyses, species distribution modelling, Bayesian statistics
